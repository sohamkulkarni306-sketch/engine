CARS BUILT
AROUND THEIR ENGINE

An engine is a promise. These five cars are what happens when that promise gets a body, a gearbox and a driver willing to test it.

V8

Ford Mustang GT

Ford Motor Company USA

The car that defined the pony car era and never really left it. The Mustang GT pairs a naturally aspirated V8 with rear wheel drive and a chassis tuned for one job: making that exhaust note feel earned every time you put your foot down.

5.0L Coyote V8Naturally aspirated · RWD
~480Horsepower
4.3s0–60 mph
163 mphTop Speed
V12

Ferrari 812 Superfast

Ferrari Maranello, Italy

One of the last naturally aspirated V12 flagships Ferrari built before turbocharging took over the lineup. It revs to a screaming 8,900 rpm a number that reads more like a jet turbine than a road car, and sounds like one too.

6.5L V12Naturally aspirated · RWD
789Horsepower
2.9s0–60 mph
211 mphTop Speed
W16

Bugatti Veyron 16.4

Bugatti Automobiles Molsheim, France

The car built to prove a single engine architecture could rewrite the top speed record books. Four turbochargers force feed sixteen cylinders arranged like two V8s fused into a W a layout that has never existed in any other production car.

8.0L Quad-Turbo W16AWD
1,001Horsepower
2.5s0–60 mph
253 mphTop Speed
Turbo Flat-4

Subaru Impreza WRX STI

Subaru Gunma, Japan

Born on rally stages, not showrooms. Subaru's turbocharged flat four "boxer" engine sits low in the chassis for a lower center of gravity, paired with symmetrical all-wheel drive built for gravel, tarmac and everything in between.

Nissan GT-R

Nissan Tochigi, Japan

Nicknamed "Godzilla" for a reason: a hand built twin turbo V6, launch control and all wheel drive engineered to embarrass cars costing three times as much on any track in the world.
